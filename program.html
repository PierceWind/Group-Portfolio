BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN INFORMATION SYSTEM







Definition of

BSIS


The Bachelor of Science in Information Systems (BSIS) is a four-year degree program that focuses on the design and implementation of solutions that combine information technology with business operations. Students will learn how to collect, filter, process, produce, and distribute data using hardware and software tools.

The Bachelor of Science in Information Systems degree focuses on the design, development, and implementation of information and communications technology (ICT) resources in order to ensure that information systems applications may be used in a variety of industries (business, education, media and entertainment, healthcare, government, and service).

BS in Information Systems used to be known as BS in Information Management, as previously stated (BSIM). Although the name of the course was changed in 2006, some schools continue to use the previous term.

Importance of

BSIS


The Bachelor of Science in Information Science (BSIS) program can help you prepare for a rewarding career in a fast growing sector. You'll learn about the creation and management of information content, the characteristics and needs of people who generate, consume, and use information, and the technologies that support the creation, management, preservation, and transmission of information as an information science major.

BSIS graduates are aware of the various methods in which information can be created, communicated, stored, and/or transformed to benefit people, organizations, and society. In contemporary knowledge-based culture, BSIS graduates have practical skills for evaluating, processing, and managing information, as well as building and administering information systems.
